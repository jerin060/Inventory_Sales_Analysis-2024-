Project overview

This project builds a fully functional relational database for a textile company, populated with one year of realistic operational data including seasonal festival patterns. Seven advanced SQL analyses were performed covering revenue trends, inventory health, supplier reconciliation, buyer behavior, and data quality validation — the core responsibilities of an MIS Executive role in the textile industry.

Key findings

Eid ul-Fitr season (March–April) generated the highest monthly revenue of the year, with March alone recording nearly 3× the revenue of July — the slowest month.
Grey Fabric 120 GSM and Cotton Yarn 20/1 were consistently the top two revenue-generating products across all seasons, together accounting for over 45% of annual revenue.
Delta Fabrics and Star Fashion Ltd were the top two buyers, contributing approximately 40% of total annual sales between them.
Winter peak (November–December) showed stronger sustained revenue than the Eid ul-Adha period, driven by heavy fabric and yarn demand for cold-weather garments.
Summer slowdown (July–August) saw transaction volume drop by approximately 65% compared to peak months — a critical planning window for stock replenishment.

Recommendations

1
Increase Vat Dye Black and Reactive Dye Red stock before the next Eid season — both are currently below reorder level and demand peaks sharply in March.
2
Use the July–August slowdown period to run a clearance on slow-moving accessories (Weaving Needle Sets, Bobbins) and reduce warehousing cost.
3
Diversify buyer base — over-reliance on two buyers (Delta Fabrics, Star Fashion Ltd) creates revenue risk if either reduces orders.
4
Automate the low stock alert query to run every Monday morning and send results to the procurement team via email export.
